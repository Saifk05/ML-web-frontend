What is DDoS?

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming the target with a flood of Internet traffic. DDoS attacks can cause severe downtime, loss of service, and significant financial damage.

The Impact of DDoS Attacks

Business Downtime

On average, a DDoS attack can cause hours of downtime, leading to significant revenue losses and reputational damage.

Financial Costs

DDoS attacks can cost businesses up to $40,000 per hour in mitigation efforts, lost productivity, and recovery expenses.

Security Vulnerabilities

Prolonged attacks can serve as distractions, allowing hackers to exploit system vulnerabilities and steal sensitive data.

Loss of Customer Trust

Repeated attacks can erode customer trust, causing long-term damage to brand reputation and client relationships.
